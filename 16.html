Lesson 16 · New pattern
A Visit to a Traditional Market
A quick browse that turns into a full afternoon — and the decisions that arise from it
New this lesson -기로 하다 — Decided to do — committed, deliberate decision
Reading text — self-talk with dialogue
오늘은 집 근처 전통 시장에 가기로 했어요. 원래는 그냥 구경만 할 계획이었는데 막상 가보니까 너무 재미있어서 결국 이것저것 사게 됐어요. 한 가게 앞에서 주인 아주머니가 먼저 말을 걸었어요.
[o.nɯ.ɾɯn tɕip̚ kɯn.tɕʰʌ tɕʌn.tʰoŋ ɕi.dʑaŋ.e ka.ɡi.ɾo hɛ.s͈ʌ.jo | wʌl.lɛ.nɯn kɯ.njaŋ ku.ɡjʌŋ.man hal kje.hwe.ɡi.ʌn.nɯn.de mak̚.s͈aŋ ka.bo.ni.k͈a nʌ.mu tɕɛ.mi.i.s͈ʌ.sʌ kjʌl.ɡuk̚ i.ɡʌt̚.t͈ɕʌ.ɡʌt̚ sa.ɡe dwɛ.s͈ʌ.jo | han ka.ɡe a.pʰe.sʌ tɕu.in a.dʑu.mʌ.ni.ɡa mʌn.dʑʌ ma.ɾɯl kʌ.ɾʌ.s͈ʌ.jo]
아주머니어서 오세요! 오늘 오징어가 정말 신선해요. 한번 맛보고 가세요.
[ʌ.sʌ o.se.jo | o.nɯl o.dʑiŋ.ʌ.ɡa tɕʌŋ.mal ɕin.sʌn.ɦɛ.jo | han.bʌn mat̚.p͈o.ɡo ka.se.jo]
나감사해요. 근데 너무 많이 사면 못 먹을 것 같아서요.
[kam.sa.ɦɛ.jo | kɯn.de nʌ.mu ma.ni sa.mjʌn mon mʌ.ɡɯl ɡʌt̚ ka.tʰa.sʌ.jo]
아주머니그럼 조금만 사세요. 맛만 봐도 돼요.
[kɯ.ɾʌm tɕo.ɡɯm.man sa.se.jo | man.man bwa.do dwɛ.jo]
맛을 보고 나서 결국 오징어를 사기로 했어요. 시장을 돌아다니다 보면 자꾸 새로운 가게가 눈에 들어와요. 이런 데 자주 오면 자연스럽게 단골이 되게 돼요. 오늘 시장에 오길 잘했다고 느꼈어요. 다음에는 친구도 데려오기로 했어요.
[ma.s͈ɯl bo.ɡo na.sʌ kjʌl.ɡuk̚ o.dʑiŋ.ʌ.ɾɯl sa.ɡi.ɾo hɛ.s͈ʌ.jo | ɕi.dʑaŋ.ɯl to.ɾa.da.ni.da bo.mjʌn tɕa.k͈u sɛ.ɾo.un ka.ɡe.ɡa nu.ne dɯ.ɾʌ.wa.jo | i.ɾʌn de tɕa.dʑu o.mjʌn tɕa.jʌn.sɯ.ɾʌp̚.k͈e tan.ɡo.ɾi dwe.ɡe dwɛ.jo | o.nɯl ɕi.dʑaŋ.e o.ɡil tɕal.ɦɛt̚.t͈a.ɡo nɯ.k͈jʌ.s͈ʌ.jo | ta.ɯ.me.nɯn tɕʰin.ɡu.do te.ɾjʌ.o.ɡi.ɾo hɛ.s͈ʌ.jo]
Today I decided to go to the traditional market near my house. Originally I was planning to just look around, but once I actually got there it was so interesting that I ended up buying this and that. In front of one stall the owner — an auntie — spoke to me first. / Auntie: Welcome! The squid is really fresh today. Please have a taste before you go. / Me: Thank you. But if I buy too much I don't think I'll be able to eat it all. / Auntie: Then just buy a little. Just tasting is fine. / After tasting it I decided to buy the squid after all. As you keep walking around a market, new stalls keep catching your eye. If you come to a place like this often, you naturally end up becoming a regular. I felt that I was glad I came to the market today. Next time I decided to bring a friend too.

★ New this lesson
-기로 하다
Decided to do — committed decision
Today I decided to go to the traditional market
★ new — more decisive than -(으)려고 하다 (intention). 가기로 했어요 = I have committed to going.
-다 보면
As you keep doing — things naturally happen
As you keep walking around, new stalls catch your eye
Now in consolidation — used naturally in context.
-면 + -게 되다
Condition → natural habitual outcome
If you come here often, you naturally end up becoming a regular
자연스럽게 = naturally. Often softens the sense of inevitability.
-다고 느끼다
Feel / sense that (personal, subjective)
I felt that I was glad I came to the market today
오길 잘했다 = it was good that I came — a very useful fixed expression.

Exercises
A
Fill in the blanks
Complete, then reveal
오늘부터 매일 아침 운동하___ 했어요.
From today I decided to exercise every morning.
오늘부터 매일 아침 운동하기로 했어요.
-기로 하다 = committed decision. Compare: 운동하려고 해요 = I intend to exercise (less committed).
시장을 돌아다니___ 새로운 가게가 자꾸 눈에 들어와요.
As you keep wandering the market, new stalls keep catching your eye.
시장을 돌아다니다 보면 새로운 가게가 자꾸 눈에 들어와요.
-다 보면 = as you keep doing X, Y naturally keeps happening. 눈에 들어오다 = to catch one's eye.
이런 곳에 자주 오면 자연스럽게 단골이 되___ 돼요.
If you come to a place like this often, you naturally become a regular.
이런 곳에 자주 오면 자연스럽게 단골이 되게 돼요.
-면 = recurring condition. -게 되다 = natural unplanned outcome.
오늘 시장에 오길 잘했___ 느꼈어요.
I felt that I was glad I came to the market today.
오늘 시장에 오길 잘했다고 느꼈어요.
-다고 느끼다 = feel/sense that. 오길 잘했다 = it was good that I came.

Questions about the text
Answer before revealing
화자의 원래 계획은 뭐였어요? 실제로는 어떻게 됐어요?
What was the original plan? What actually happened?
그냥 구경만 할 계획이었는데 재미있어서 이것저것 사게 됐어요.
-(으)ㄹ 계획이었다 = had a plan. -게 됐어요 = ended up buying (unplanned).

-다고 했다 = reported statement. -라고 했다 = reported command. Good consolidation of lesson 14.

C
Translation
One or two patterns per sentence
1. I decided to go to the traditional market instead of the supermarket.
슈퍼마켓 대신 전통 시장에 가기로 했어요.
-기로 하다 = committed decision. 대신 = instead of.
Also acceptable
마트 대신 재래시장에 가기로 마음먹었어요.
2. As you keep going to this market, you naturally get to know the stall owners.
이 시장에 자꾸 가다 보면 가게 주인들을 자연스럽게 알게 돼요.
-다 보면 = as you keep going. -게 되다 = naturally come to know.
Also acceptable
자주 가다 보면 주인들과 친해지게 돼요.
3. After today I decided to come to the traditional market every weekend.
오늘 이후로 매주 주말에 전통 시장에 오기로 했어요.
-기로 하다 = decided. A firm commitment about future behaviour.
Also acceptable
앞으로는 주말마다 시장에 가기로 마음먹었어요.
4. I felt that a market like this was much more interesting than I had expected.
이런 시장이 생각보다 훨씬 재미있다고 느꼈어요.
-다고 느끼다 = felt that. 생각보다 = more than expected. 훨씬 = much / far more.
Also acceptable
전통 시장이 이렇게 재미있을 줄 몰랐다고 느꼈어요.
5. I decided not to buy too much this time.
이번에는 너무 많이 사지 않기로 했어요.
-지 않기로 하다 = decide not to. The negative of -기로 하다.
Also acceptable
다음부터는 과식하지 않기로 했어요.
6. As you keep chatting with the owner, you naturally become friends.
주인이랑 이야기하다 보면 자연스럽게 친해지게 돼요.
-다 보면 = as you keep doing. 친해지다 = to become close. -게 되다 = naturally come to.
Also acceptable
단골이 되다 보면 주인이랑 친해져요.

Review — Lessons 14 to 16
A market outing that brings together reported speech, “keep doing,” and committed decisions
Reviewing -다고/-(으)라고 하다 · -다 보면 · -기로 하다
Reading text
지난주에 친구가 새로 생긴 시장에 같이 가자고 했어요. 처음에는 그냥 구경만 하기로 했는데 막상 가보니까 너무 재미있었어요. 상인이 이것저것 맛보라고 해서 다 먹어봤어요. 시장을 돌아다니다 보면 자꾸 새로운 게 눈에 들어와요. 친구가 여기 자주 오다 보면 단골이 된다고 했어요. 정말 그런 것 같아요. 자주 갈수록 더 정이 들어요. 다음 주에도 또 가기로 했어요.
[tɕi.nan.dʑu.e tɕʰin.ɡu.ɡa sɛ.ɾo sɛŋ.ɡin ɕi.dʑaŋ.e ka.tʰi ka.dʑa.ɡo hɛ.s͈ʌ.jo | tɕʰʌ.ɯ.me.nɯn kɯ.njaŋ ku.ɡjʌŋ.man ha.ɡi.ɾo hɛn.nɯn.de mak̚.s͈aŋ ka.bo.ni.k͈a nʌ.mu tɕɛ.mi.i.s͈ʌ.s͈ʌ.jo | saŋ.i.ni i.ɡʌt̚.t͈ɕʌ.ɡʌt̚ mat̚.p͈o.ɾa.ɡo hɛ.sʌ ta mʌ.ɡʌ.bwa.s͈ʌ.jo | ɕi.dʑaŋ.ɯl to.ɾa.da.ni.da bo.mjʌn tɕa.k͈u sɛ.ɾo.un ɡe nu.ne dɯ.ɾʌ.wa.jo | tɕʰin.ɡu.ɡa jʌ.ɡi tɕa.dʑu o.da bo.mjʌn tan.ɡo.ɾi dwen.da.ɡo hɛ.s͈ʌ.jo | tɕʌŋ.mal kɯ.ɾʌn ɡʌt̚ ka.tʰa.jo | tɕa.dʑu kal.s͈u.ɾok̚ tʌ tɕʌŋ.i dɯ.ɾʌ.jo | ta.ɯm dʑu.e.do t͈o ka.ɡi.ɾo hɛ.s͈ʌ.jo]
Last week a friend suggested we go together to a newly-opened market. At first we decided to just look around, but once we actually got there it was so much fun. The vendor told us to taste this and that, so we tried everything. As you keep wandering the market, new things keep catching your eye. My friend said if you come here often you become a regular. I really think that's true. The more often you go, the more attached you get. We decided to go again next week too.

-다고 / -(으)라고 하다
Reported speech — review
The vendor told us to taste this and that
Statement -다고, command -(으)라고, suggestion -자고.
-다 보면
If you keep doing — review
If you come often, you become a regular
Forward-looking. Contrast -다 보니까 (past discovery).
-기로 하다
Decided to — review
We decided to go again next week
Committed decision. Negative: -지 않기로 하다.
-(으)ㄹ수록
The more… the more — review
The more you go, the more attached you get
Fuller form: 갈수록 / 가면 갈수록.
