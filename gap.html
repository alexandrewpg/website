Gap-Fix Lesson · Month 2

정리하기 — Tidying Up

Six errors from your Month 2 evaluation, isolated and drilled. Small lesson, targeted fixes.

복습 · Review
01 What came up
긑내서 / 긑났도
끝내서 / 끝냈지만
Spelling: the syllable is 끝 (final consonant ㅌ), not 긑. This isn't a valid Korean syllable block.
몰으갰어요
모르겠어요 / 몰랐어요
모르다 is a 르-irregular verb. Its forms don't follow the regular pattern — worth memorizing as a set (drilled below).
할려고 했어요 (for "decided")
하기로 했어요
-려고 하다 = intend to. -기로 하다 = decided to. When the English says "decided," reach for -기로 하다.
크로즈 했어요
문을 닫았어요
"Close (a shop)" is 문을 닫다 (lit. "close the door") — not an English loanword.
결정 했어요 (for "felt worried")
걱정이 됐어요
걱정이 되다 = to become worried. 결정하다 means "to decide" — different word entirely.
나는 말한것을
내가 한 말을
Inside a relative clause, the embedded subject usually takes 이/가, not 는. "What I said" = 내가 한 말 (the words that I said).
02 모르다 — the irregular verb

모르다 drops 르 and adds ㄹㄹ before a vowel ending. This happens to all 르-irregular verbs (다르다, 빠르다, 부르다...).

FormKoreanMeaning
Present몰라요don't know
Past몰랐어요didn't know
Present (formal tone)모르겠어요I'm not sure / I don't know
+ 줄 (embedded)-는 줄 몰랐어요didn't know that...

알다 (to know) is regular but drops ㄹ before ㅂ, 는, ㅅ — compare: 알아요 / 알았어요 / 아는 (not 알는).

03 결정 vs 의도 — Decision vs Intention

-기로 하다 → a firm decision, often with an outcome

-(으)려고 하다 → a plan or intention, may or may not happen

그만두기로 했어요. — I decided to quit. (done deal)

그만두려고 해요. — I'm thinking of quitting. (still forming)

04 Translation drill

Write your answer, then reveal to check. Every sentence recycles today's six errors.

1. I decided to quit smoking.
담배를 끊기로 했어요.
끊다 = to quit (habits); 기로 했어요 = decided
2. I'm not sure if the museum is closed today.
오늘 박물관이 문을 닫았는지 잘 모르겠어요.
문을 닫다 = to close (a place); 모르겠어요 = I'm not sure
3. Even though I finished my homework, I still felt worried about the test.
숙제를 끝냈지만 시험 때문에 그래도 걱정이 됐어요.
끝냈지만 (transitive + contrast); 걱정이 됐어요 (became worried)
4. I didn't know she had already left the company.
그녀가 벌써 회사를 그만둔 줄 몰랐어요.
벌써 (already) not 아직; 그만두다 = to quit (a job)
5. What I decided was to travel alone.
제가 결정한 것은 혼자 여행하는 거예요.
Note: 결정하다 IS correct here — this sentence is actually about deciding, not worrying. Contrast with error #5 above.
6. I don't remember what she said yesterday.
어제 그녀가 한 말이 기억이 안 나요.
그녀가 한 말 = the words she said (relative clause, 가 not 는 inside)
05 Quick check
1. 가게가 벌써 ___ 몰랐어요. (I didn't know the store had already closed.)
닫은 줄 몰랐어요 — past completed action + 줄 모르다.
2. 내일 여행을 ___ 했어요. (I decided to travel tomorrow.)
가기로 했어요 — a firm decision, not just an intention.
3. 어제 ___ 말이 정말 도움이 됐어요. (The words she said yesterday really helped.)
그녀가 한 말 — subject inside a relative clause takes 이/가.
4. 숙제를 ___ 지만 기분이 안 좋았어요. (Even though I finished my homework, I didn't feel good.)
끝냈지만 — 숙제를 has an object marker, so the transitive verb 끝내다 is needed.
