Lesson 14 · New pattern
Two Friends at a Street Food Festival (2)
Festival rules, what the staff said, and the famous tteokbokki stall
New this lesson -다고 말하다 / -(으)라고 하다 — Reported speech — statement and command
Reading text — dialogue (continued from lesson 13)
준서행사 직원이 뭐라고 했어? 입장료 같은 거 있었어?
[hɛŋ.sa dʑi.ɡwʌ.ni mwʌ.ɾa.ɡo hɛ.s͈ʌ | ip̚.t͈ɕaŋ.njo ɡa.tʰɯn ɡʌ i.s͈ʌ.s͈ʌ]
유나입구에서 직원이 음식은 행사 구역 안에서만 먹어야 한다고 말했어. 그리고 줄을 설 때 앞에 끼어들지 말라고 했어.
[ip̚.k͈u.e.sʌ dʑi.ɡwʌ.ni ɯm.ɕi.ɡɯn hɛŋ.sa ɡu.jʌɡ a.ne.sʌ.man mʌ.ɡʌ.ja han.da.ɡo mal.ɦɛ.s͈ʌ | kɯ.ɾi.ɡo dʑu.ɾɯl sʌl t͈ɛ a.pʰe k͈i.ʌ.dɯl.dʑi mal.la.ɡo hɛ.s͈ʌ]
준서어떤 가게가 제일 인기가 있는지 알았어?
[ʌ.t͈ʌn ɡa.ɡe.ɡa dʑe.il in.ɡi.ɡa in.nɯn.dʑi a.ɾa.s͈ʌ]
유나몰랐는데 가다 보니까 한 가게 앞에 줄이 엄청 길었어. 떡볶이 파는 가게였는데 냄새만 맡고도 먹고 싶어질 뻔했어.
[mol.lan.nɯn.de ɡa.da bo.ni.k͈a han ɡa.ɡe a.pʰe dʑu.ɾi ʌm.tɕʰʌŋ ɡi.ɾʌ.s͈ʌ | t͈ʌk̚.p͈o.k͈i pʰa.nɯn ɡa.ɡe.jʌn.nɯn.de nɛm.sɛ.man mat̚.k͈o.do mʌk̚.k͈o ɕi.pʰʌ.dʑil p͈ʌn.ɦɛ.s͈ʌ]
준서나도 그 축제 다음에 꼭 가야겠다.
[na.do kɯ tɕʰuk̚.t͈ɕe da.ɯ.me k͈ok̚ k͈a.ja.ɡet̚.t͈a]
Joonser: What did the event staff say? Was there an entrance fee? / Yuna: At the entrance a staff member said the food could only be eaten inside the event zone. And they told us not to cut in line when queuing. / Joonser: Did you know which stall was most popular? / Yuna: I didn't, but as I walked along I noticed one stall had an incredibly long queue. It was a tteokbokki stall — just smelling it I nearly gave in and wanted to eat immediately. / Joonser: I should definitely go to that festival next time.

★ New this lesson
-다고 말하다
Reported statement — said that
They said the food could only be eaten inside the event zone
★ new — the quoted clause stays in plain form (-다), not polite (-요).
★ New this lesson
-(으)라고 하다
Reported command — told to / told not to
They told us not to cut in line when queuing
★ new — negative command: -지 말다 → -지 말라고. Affirmative: verb stem + -(으)라고.
-다 보니까
As I kept doing — I noticed / discovered
As I walked along I noticed one stall had an incredibly long queue
-다 보니까 = past discovery. -다 보면 = forward-looking outcome (lesson 15).
-(으)ㄹ 뻔하다
Almost happened — consolidation
Just from the smell I nearly gave in and wanted to eat
From lesson 13 — notice how naturally it recurs in context.

Fill in the blanks
Complete, then reveal
직원이 음식은 안에서만 먹어야 한___ 말했어요.
(reported statement — plain form + -다고)
직원이 음식은 안에서만 먹어야 한다고 말했어요.
-다고 말하다 = reported statement. Verb inside the quote stays in plain form: 한다, not 해요.
줄에서 앞에 끼어들지 말___ 했어요.
(reported negative command — -지 말라고)
줄에서 앞에 끼어들지 말라고 했어요.
Negative reported command: -지 말다 → -지 말라고. Affirmative: 끼어들라고 했어요.
걷___ 보니까 한 가게 앞에 줄이 엄청 길었어요.
(as I kept walking I noticed — -다 보니까)
걷다 보니까 한 가게 앞에 줄이 엄청 길었어요.
-다 보니까 = as I kept doing, I discovered. Attach -다 directly to the verb stem.
냄새만 맡고도 먹고 싶어질 ___했어요.
(almost wanted to eat — but managed not to)
냄새만 맡고도 먹고 싶어질 뻔했어요.
-(으)ㄹ 뻔했어요 in consolidation. 먹고 싶어지다 = to come to want to eat.

직원이 음식에 대해 뭐라고 말했어요?
What did the staff member say about food?
음식은 행사 구역 안에서만 먹어야 한다고 말했어요.
-다고 말하다 = reported statement. Plain form inside the quote.
Also acceptable
행사 밖에서는 음식을 먹으면 안 된다고 했어요.
유나는 어떻게 인기 있는 가게를 발견했어요?
How did Yuna discover the popular stall?
가다 보니까 한 가게 앞에 줄이 엄청 길게 서 있었어요.
-다 보니까 = as she kept walking, she discovered. The discovery happened naturally.
Also acceptable
걷다 보니까 사람이 많이 모인 가게를 발견했어요.

Translation
One or two patterns per sentence
1. The staff member said that photos were not allowed inside.
직원이 안에서는 사진을 찍으면 안 된다고 말했어요.
-다고 말하다 = reported statement. -으면 안 된다 = must not / not allowed.
Also acceptable
직원이 내부에서는 촬영이 금지됐다고 했어요.
2. The guide told everyone to move to the left side.
가이드가 모두 왼쪽으로 이동하라고 했어요.
-(으)라고 하다 = reported command. 이동하다 → 이동하라고.
Also acceptable
가이드가 왼쪽으로 가라고 했어요.
3. As I kept tasting, I gradually got fuller and fuller.
먹다 보니까 배가 점점 더 불러졌어요.
-다 보니까 = as I kept eating, I discovered. 불러지다 = to become full.
Also acceptable
계속 먹다 보니까 더 이상 못 먹을 것 같았어요.
4. My friend said she would come, but she didn't show up.
친구가 온다고 했는데 나타나지 않았어요.
-다고 하다 = reported statement (informal). 나타나다 = to appear / show up.
Also acceptable
친구가 올 거라고 했는데 결국 오지 않았어요.

Lesson 15 · New pattern
A Solo Trip to a New City
Unfamiliar alleyways, a cosy café discovery, and how things gradually feel less foreign
New this lesson -다 보면 — If you keep doing — an outcome eventually follows
Reading text
처음 가보는 도시에 혼자 여행을 갔어요. 처음에는 낯설어 보였던 골목들이 걷다 보면 점점 친근해져요. 지도를 보지 않고 걸을수록 더 재미있는 곳을 발견하게 되는 것 같아요. 어떤 카페 앞을 지나가다가 안이 정말 아늑해 보였어요. 들어가 보니까 아까 지나쳤던 유명한 카페라는 걸 나중에 알게 됐어요. 낯선 곳도 다 보다 보면 언젠가는 익숙해지는 것 같아요. 이번 여행으로 혼자 여행하는 것도 충분히 즐길 수 있다는 걸 알게 됐어요.
I went on a solo trip to a city I had never been to. Alleyways that seemed unfamiliar at first gradually become familiar as you keep walking. The more you walk without looking at a map, the more interesting places you seem to find. I was walking past a café and the inside looked really cosy. When I went in, I found out later that it was a famous café I had passed by earlier. Even unfamiliar places — if you keep looking around, you eventually become familiar with them. Through this trip I came to realise that you can enjoy solo travelling well enough on your own.

★ New this lesson
-다 보면
If you keep doing — outcome eventually follows
If you keep walking, things gradually become familiar
★ new — forward-looking. -다 보니까 (lesson 14) looks back at a past discovery.
-(으)ㄹ수록
The more… the more (proportional)
The more you walk without a map, the more interesting places you find
걸으면 걸을수록 is the fuller form — both are natural.
-아/어 보이다 + -았/었던
Seemed (past state, now changed)
The alleyways that had seemed unfamiliar gradually became familiar
-았/었던 = past-state modifier; implies the state has since changed.
-다는 걸 알게 되다
Came to find out / realise that
I found out later that it was a famous café
알게 됐어요 = came to know through experience, not by being told directly.

Lesson 16 · New pattern
A Visit to a Traditional Market
A quick browse that turns into a full afternoon — and the decisions that arise from it
New this lesson -기로 하다 — Decided to do — committed, deliberate decision
Reading text — self-talk with dialogue
오늘은 집 근처 전통 시장에 가기로 했어요. 원래는 그냥 구경만 할 계획이었는데 막상 가보니까 너무 재미있어서 결국 이것저것 사게 됐어요. 한 가게 앞에서 주인 아주머니가 먼저 말을 걸었어요.
아주머니어서 오세요! 오늘 오징어가 정말 신선해요. 한번 맛보고 가세요.
나감사해요. 근데 너무 많이 사면 못 먹을 것 같아서요.
아주머니그럼 조금만 사세요. 맛만 봐도 돼요.
맛을 보고 나서 결국 오징어를 사기로 했어요. 시장을 돌아다니다 보면 자꾸 새로운 가게가 눈에 들어와요. 이런 데 자주 오면 자연스럽게 단골이 되게 돼요. 오늘 시장에 오길 잘했다고 느꼈어요. 다음에는 친구도 데려오기로 했어요.
Today I decided to go to the traditional market near my house. Originally I was planning to just look around, but once I actually got there it was so interesting that I ended up buying this and that. In front of one stall the owner — an auntie — spoke to me first. / Auntie: Welcome! The squid is really fresh today. Please have a taste before you go. / Me: Thank you. But if I buy too much I don't think I'll be able to eat it all. / Auntie: Then just buy a little. Just tasting is fine. / After tasting it I decided to buy the squid after all. As you keep walking around a market, new stalls keep catching your eye. If you come to a place like this often, you naturally end up becoming a regular. I felt that I was glad I came to the market today. Next time I decided to bring a friend too.

★ New this lesson
-기로 하다
Decided to do — committed decision
Today I decided to go to the traditional market
★ new — more decisive than -(으)려고 하다 (intention). 가기로 했어요 = I have committed to going.
-다 보면
As you keep doing — things naturally happen
As you keep walking around, new stalls catch your eye
Now in consolidation — used naturally in context.
-면 + -게 되다
Condition → natural habitual outcome
If you come here often, you naturally end up becoming a regular
자연스럽게 = naturally. Often softens the sense of inevitability.
-다고 느끼다
Feel / sense that (personal, subjective)
I felt that I was glad I came to the market today
오길 잘했다 = it was good that I came — a very useful fixed expression.
