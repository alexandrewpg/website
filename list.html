What the host's message settles

  • Water is sorted. The host supplies jugs of filtered water for drinking and cooking, and will bring refills if you text. No need to haul water — just bring personal bottles (a non-metal one for the sauna). One less heavy thing for the 700 m walk.
  • The kitchen is stocked. Cookware, dishes, utensils, a French press, plus spices, oil, herbal teas, honey, and free organic oats for porridge. Bring only the extras you actually want: your own ground coffee, a sharp knife, maybe a cast-iron pan.
  • Skip the plug-in cooler. It runs off the solar system and the host warns it can drain the battery on overcast days — and your forecast is rain both days. Bring a normal cooler with block ice.
  • Charge at the sauna, not the cabin. Cabin power is tight (run lights at 25%, never draw below 20% — press "B" on the panel to check). There's a proper electrical plug at the sauna for devices and high-use items; charge your phone and the hearing-aid case there. Bring one power bank as backup.
  • Firewood is provided. A bin by the stove, more stacked outside. The first firepit crate each day is included; extra crates are $5 on the honour system, so bring a little cash or e-transfer.
  • Mind the bake oven on a warm day. It's great for pizza or biscuits but takes an hour-plus to heat and roasts the cabin. Use the propane deck stove for quick meals and morning coffee.
